I am a student at the University of Manitoba in the Computer Science and Mathematics Joint Program. I am a certified AWS Cloud Practitioner and Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA).

My journey in computer networking began about eight years ago. As a young kid, I wanted to download a few songs to my tablet, but we didn’t have a Wi-Fi network at the time. We had only a desktop connected to the internet via an Ethernet cable. I spent hours exploring the Windows Control Panel and searching online until I finally figured out how to share the desktop’s internet connection with my tablet. That experience sparked my passion for discovering how computer networks work. I had even more fun later on when I configured my tablet as an access point, allowing my brother and me to play online games together.

I eventually enrolled at IT Step Academy — the second-best decision of my life, with the first being immigrating to Canada. At the academy, I learned a lot about the OSI model, the inner workings of IP and non-IP networks, network infrastructure, protocols, Cisco technologies and solutions, and gained plenty of hands-on experience using Packet Tracer and advanced labs in GNS3.

During my studies, I also explored cloud and virtual networks, working with Docker networking, Hyper-V, VirtualBox, AWS, and a bit of Azure. I gained experience configuring Windows Servers, as well as Linux systems such as Ubuntu and CentOS.
